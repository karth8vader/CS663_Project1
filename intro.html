Introduction

Sign language is a vital communication tool, not only for those who are deaf or hard of hearing, but also as a means of fostering inclusivity and bridging communities. Teaching children sign language early in life creates opportunities for empathy, understanding, and meaningful interaction with diverse groups of people.

However, learning sign language can be intimidating for beginners. Traditional teaching methods may not always capture children’s attention or provide the immediate feedback they need to improve. This is where computer vision can play an important role. By analyzing hand shapes and gestures in real-time, computer vision systems can provide interactive and engaging lessons that respond instantly to a learner’s progress.

Figure 2: Children engaging with sign language as part of inclusive education (illustrative).

Why Use Computer Vision?

Computer vision enables real-time gesture recognition, making learning sign language more engaging and interactive for children. This approach blends technology with education to support inclusivity and accessibility.
