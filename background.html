Literature Review & Background

Research into sign language recognition has grown significantly in recent years. Advances in computer vision and machine learning have led to tools capable of recognizing hand gestures with increasing accuracy. Some of these systems have been applied in education, offering interactive platforms for learners.

Despite this progress, most existing tools are designed for general audiences or adult learners. Few systems directly target children, who may require simplified interfaces, gamified learning, and more accessible feedback mechanisms. These gaps highlight the need for child-focused solutions that integrate both educational psychology and technical robustness.

Research Gaps
